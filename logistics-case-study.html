The Chaos of "Manual" Dispatch

Dispatch managers and small businesses were operating in a "Black Box." Deliveries were managed via spreadsheets and WhatsApp, leading to lost packages, incorrect cost estimations, and zero real-time visibility for the end customer.

The challenge was to build an end-to-end system that automated the three pillars of last-mile logistics: Scheduling, Estimation, and Tracking.

The Core Solution

An integrated Web & Mobile ecosystem that centralizes the order lifecycle, providing drivers with optimized routes and managers with a real-time "Control Tower" view.

The Discovery: Mapping the Journey

To build this from scratch, I mapped the entire logistics journey from the moment an order is placed to the final "Proof of Delivery" (POD).

  • 01. User Research: Interviewed 15+ dispatchers to find that "Cost Estimation" was their biggest point of friction and revenue leakage.
  • 02. Feature Prioritization: Using the MoSCoW method, I prioritized Real-time Tracking and Automated Billing over secondary features like "In-app Chat" for the MVP.
  • 03. Tech Specs: Defined the logic for the "Cost Estimation Engine" based on distance (Google Maps API), weight, and vehicle type.

Solving the "Estimation" Logic

A critical technical hurdle was preventing revenue loss from inaccurate pricing. I collaborated with engineering to build a dynamic pricing algorithm. We integrated weight-class triggers and distance-based tolling. During pilot testing, this automation recovered 12% in "hidden costs" that were previously missed by manual calculations.
