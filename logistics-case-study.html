Logistics & Courier Management Platform

Full case study showing end-to-end product ownership, problem solving, and impact.

Problem / Context

Dispatch managers and small businesses lacked a centralized platform to schedule pickups, track deliveries in real-time, and calculate costs efficiently. Manual tracking caused delays, errors, and poor customer visibility. The platform was designed to streamline operations and improve accountability.

Role / Scope

I led product definition, user journey mapping, and prioritization of key features. Worked closely with engineering to define functional requirements and ensure smooth delivery of both web and mobile applications.

Approach / Solution

  1. User Discovery: Conducted interviews with dispatch managers and small business owners to understand operational challenges.
  2. Feature Definition: Designed features for pickup scheduling, real-time tracking, cost estimation, and dashboard visibility.
  3. Prioritization: Applied impact vs. effort framework to focus on high-value operational features first.
  4. Design & Specifications: Created detailed user stories, acceptance criteria, and wireframes for engineering and design teams.
  5. Iterative Delivery: Ran development cycles with continuous feedback and refinements.
  6. Validation & Refinement: Early testing ensured usability, efficiency, and reliability before going live.

Outcome / Impact

  • Platform is about to go live with early testing completed.
  • Improves operational efficiency for dispatch managers and small businesses.
  • Reduces manual tracking errors and simplifies delivery scheduling.
  • Expected 25–35% reduction in manual errors and significant time savings.

Lessons Learned

- Engaging end-users early uncovers critical operational challenges.
- Iterative development allows for refinement before full-scale launch.
- Clear dashboards and tracking features are essential for operational transparency.
