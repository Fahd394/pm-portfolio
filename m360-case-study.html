M360 Suite – Multi-Module Marketing Platform

Full case study showing end-to-end product ownership, problem solving, and impact.

← Back to Portfolio

Problem / Context

Marketing teams struggled with inefficiencies, duplicated work, and limited visibility when managing campaigns across multiple platforms. M360 Suite was built to provide a unified, scalable solution, combining ads management, SEO, community management, email automation, social scheduling, and automated content generation in a single platform.

Role / Scope

I owned the product end-to-end: discovery, MVP definition, roadmap planning, stakeholder alignment, and execution with engineering and design teams. I conducted workshops with users to identify pain points, prioritized features, and guided iterative development cycles. I also worked closely with the marketing team to validate assumptions and refine the user experience.

Approach / Solution

  1. User Discovery: Conducted workshops and interviews with marketing teams to understand pain points and workflow bottlenecks.
  2. Feature Definition: Defined MVP features spanning ads management, SEO, community management, email automation, social scheduling, and automated content generation.
  3. Prioritization: Applied impact vs. effort frameworks to prioritize features that delivered maximum value quickly.
  4. Design & Specifications: Created detailed user stories, acceptance criteria, and wireframes for engineering and design teams.
  5. Iterative Delivery: Guided multiple development cycles, integrating continuous feedback from stakeholders and early adopters.
  6. Validation & Refinement: Monitored usage metrics and user feedback to refine the platform and ensure adoption.

Outcome / Impact

  • Platform is live with early adoption from target users.
  • Reduces campaign setup complexity and time for multi-channel marketing.
  • Centralizes campaign visibility and reporting across modules.
  • Anticipated 30–40% improvement in campaign setup efficiency.
  • Higher user satisfaction and adoption expected across marketing teams.

Lessons Learned

- Early user involvement and feedback is critical to avoid feature bloat.
- Clear prioritization frameworks help balance business goals with user needs.
- Structured communication with cross-functional teams ensures smoother delivery and faster iteration.
